05 · orange rules

orange is a spotlight, not a fill.

the accent color has a handful of jobs: calls-to-action, section labels, checkmarks, and emphasizing one key word per layout. it has two emphasis treatments — anchor (hero copy) and marker (body/carousel). never both in one layout.

anchor · .ink-underline
orange text with a wobbly hand-drawn underline beneath it. used for hero copy and big display moments — when the word IS the headline. feels bold, confident, brand-forward.
your business deserves a launchpad.
marker · .highlight
ink text with an orange marker stroke behind it. used in body copy, carousel slides, and annotations — when the word is inside a sentence you're reading. feels hand-annotated, zine-y.
57% of mobile users bounce after 3 seconds.
when to anchor
hero headlines, section titles, display-scale type. the emphasized word stands on its own visually. one per hero, max.
when to marker
body copy, carousel slides, tips, captions. the emphasized phrase sits inside a block of reading. one per block, max.
never both
never use anchor and marker in the same layout. pick one based on what the surrounding copy is doing.

06 · illustration

hand-drawn, inked, two-tone.

illustrations stay pure — ink on cream, never orange, never gradients. the stamp/linocut feel is what makes them read as human-made. the moment color enters, the illusion breaks.

do
  • keep everything ink on cream
  • lean into rough edges, grain, imperfection
  • use scattered dots / stars as composition
  • let silhouettes carry the character
  • use ink marks (arrows, circles, squiggles) often
don't
  • add color to illustrations
  • use gradients, drop shadows, or 3d effects
  • over-detail — iconic beats accurate
  • ground the character on a surface
  • straighten ink marks (imperfection is the point)
07 · voice & tone

earnest about the customer. playful about blaztly.

two registers, one brand. we take the customer's business seriously. we don't take ourselves too seriously. that's the split that keeps blaztly from feeling either corporate or flippant.

earnest · when speaking about the customer
sincere, encouraging, customer-centric. space metaphors used with respect, not irony. this is the voice on the hero, pricing, sales pages — anywhere the customer is the subject.
your business deserves a launchpad.
playful · when speaking about blaztly
short, punchy, likes a twist. self-aware about the absurdity of having a monkey mascot. this is the voice on instagram captions, about pages, 404s — anywhere blaztly is the subject.
built by humans. launched by monkeys.
lowercase default
all marketing — site, ig, ads, logo, hero, buttons, body — is lowercase. it's the aesthetic.
legibility wins
proper nouns keep their natural case only when legibility genuinely demands it (iOS, SaaS). otherwise lowercase them (chownow, google, webflow).
1:1 client comms
emails, dms, proposals, and direct client messages use normal sentence case. the lowercase rule is a brand-voice rule, not a texting habit.
sentence shape
short. punchy. likes a twist. periods are powerful. never ramble when a punchline will do.

09 · principles

the shortlist.

if you remember nothing else from this guide, remember these.

anti-slop
we're not anti-ai. we're anti-slop. everything we ship should look like a human made a decision.
cream + ink + one spotlight
every layout is built on cream and ink. orange is a spotlight used sparingly. warm grays handle structure.
lowercase by default
marketing is lowercase. proper nouns get normal case only when legibility demands it. client emails are normal sentence case.
cherry bomb = logo only
the display font exists for the wordmark. nowhere else. no exceptions.
illustrations stay pure
ink on cream. no orange, no gradients, no 3d. hand-drawn character or don't use it.
ink marks, often
scribbles, arrows, dashed circles, asterisks, wavy underlines. they signal "a human put this here." use them frequently.
straight layouts, crooked ink
grids, buttons, type blocks — all square and aligned. only ink-drawn marks are allowed to tilt. don't fake imperfection; place it only where it would naturally occur.
copy OR ink, never both
for any self-contained layout (carousel slide, social post, hero block), commit to one mode. don't split the viewer's attention.
show the what, sell the how
teach publicly. sell the build. our content gives away recipes. clients pay for the dinner.
earnest + playful
serious about the customer's business. not serious about our monkey. that's the voice split, everywhere.
